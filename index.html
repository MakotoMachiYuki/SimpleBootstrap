About

Contemporary Japanese society is decidedly urban. Not only do the vast majority of Japanese live in urban settings, but urban culture is transmitted throughout the country by a mass media largely concentrated in Tokyo. Young urban Japanese in particular have become known for their conspicuous consumption and their penchant for trends and fads that quickly go in and out of fashion.

Beyond the vibrant cityscapes, Japan’s natural landscapes offer breathtaking contrasts of blue and green. In summer, the sky shines a crystal-clear blue, providing a picturesque backdrop to Japan’s lush, green forests. These forests, often blanketing the mountains and countryside, are a haven for those seeking tranquility and a connection to nature. From towering bamboo groves to cedar-lined trails, the forests offer a serene escape, inviting all to experience the harmony of Japan’s rich natural beauty.
